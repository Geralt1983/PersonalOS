However, looking at these screenshots as a Product Designer, I see two specific friction points that betray the "web app" roots. We need to fix these to make it feel like a native iOS app.
The Critique & The Fix
1. The "Chin" Collision
In the Brain Dump view, the floating Deck (the navigation bar) is covering the bottom of your list ("9 of 9 thoughts"). You can't comfortably read or tap the last item.
 * The Fix: We need to add massive bottom padding to the scrolling container so the content can scroll above the deck, leaving empty space behind the navigation.
2. The Ugly Scrollbar
In the Brain Dump card and Activity list, there is a visible grey scrollbar on the right. Native apps (like Apple Notes) hide this bar or make it invisible until you scroll. It breaks the immersion.

Why this matters
Right now, you have a beautiful dashboard, but if you can't reach the bottom task because a button is covering it, you will subconsciously stop trusting the app. Adding that "empty space" at the bottom (the pb-40) gives the UI "breathing room" and makes it feel luxurious.
